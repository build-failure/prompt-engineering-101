# Prompt Engineering 101: Master AI Communication for Business

Welcome to **Prompt Engineering 101**, a comprehensive hands-on training program designed for business professionals who want to harness the power of Google Gemini and AI tools in their daily work.

## 🎯 What You'll Learn

This course teaches you how to communicate effectively with AI to:
- Draft professional emails and documents
- Summarize reports and meetings
- Analyze data and generate insights
- Create presentations and content
- Automate routine business tasks

**No technical background required** — just bring your curiosity and willingness to learn!

## 📚 Course Structure

This training consists of **12 progressive chapters**, each with:
- **Lesson**: Core concepts and techniques explained in plain language
- **Lab**: Hands-on exercises with real business scenarios
- **Validation**: Self-assessment prompts to check your work
- **Templates**: Reusable prompts you can adapt for your needs

### Beginner Track (Chapters 1-4)
Build your foundation in prompt engineering basics.

1. **[Introduction to Prompt Engineering](chapters/01-introduction/lesson.md)** (20 min)
   - What is prompt engineering and why it matters
   - How Gemini processes your requests
   - Good vs. bad prompts

2. **[Basic Prompt Structure](chapters/02-basic-structure/lesson.md)** (25 min)
   - The TCFC framework: Task, Context, Format, Constraints
   - Building well-structured prompts
   - Lab: Summarize emails and create status reports

3. **[Clarity and Specificity](chapters/03-clarity-specificity/lesson.md)** (25 min)
   - Writing clear, unambiguous instructions
   - Balancing detail and flexibility
   - Lab: Analyze data and write customer emails

4. **[Role Assignment and Persona](chapters/04-role-persona/lesson.md)** (25 min)
   - Using roles to improve AI outputs
   - Choosing the right persona for your task
   - Lab: Marketing expert, editor, and analyst roles

### Intermediate Track (Chapters 5-9)
Master advanced techniques for complex tasks.

5. **[Providing Context and Examples](chapters/05-context-examples/lesson.md)** (30 min)
   - What context to include (and what to skip)
   - Using examples to guide AI behavior
   - Lab: Audience-specific reports and emails

6. **[Separating Instructions from Data](chapters/06-instructions-data/lesson.md)** (30 min)
   - Clean prompt structure with data separation
   - Formatting techniques for clarity
   - Lab: Analyze customer feedback and meeting notes

7. **[Output Formatting and Structure](chapters/07-output-formatting/lesson.md)** (30 min)
   - Requesting specific formats (lists, tables, paragraphs)
   - Ensuring consistent outputs
   - Lab: Meeting agendas and comparison tables

8. **[Step-by-Step Reasoning](chapters/08-step-by-step/lesson.md)** (30 min)
   - Chain of thought technique
   - Breaking down complex problems
   - Lab: Decision analysis and vendor evaluation

9. **[Few-Shot Learning with Examples](chapters/09-few-shot-learning/lesson.md)** (30 min)
   - Teaching AI through examples
   - Maintaining consistent style and tone
   - Lab: Brand voice and company formatting

### Advanced Track (Chapters 10-12)
Tackle complex workflows and ensure quality.

10. **[Reducing Hallucinations and Improving Accuracy](chapters/10-accuracy-hallucinations/lesson.md)** (35 min)
    - Understanding AI limitations
    - Techniques for factual accuracy
    - Lab: Fact-checking and validation

11. **[Multi-Step Workflows and Prompt Chaining](chapters/11-multi-step-workflows/lesson.md)** (35 min)
    - Breaking tasks into sequences
    - Designing effective workflows
    - Lab: Report → Summary → Email → Presentation

12. **[Troubleshooting and Debugging Prompts](chapters/12-troubleshooting/lesson.md)** (35 min)
    - Common problems and solutions
    - Systematic debugging process
    - Lab: Fix failing prompts and optimize results
