# Email Prompt Templates

Reusable prompt templates for common email tasks. Copy, customize the [placeholders], and paste into Gemini.

---

## Follow-Up Emails

### After Meeting
```
Draft a professional follow-up email to [RECIPIENT NAME and TITLE] after [TYPE OF MEETING] on [DATE].

Thank them for [SPECIFIC THING DISCUSSED].
Summarize these key points from our discussion:
- [KEY POINT 1]
- [KEY POINT 2]
- [KEY POINT 3]

Confirm next steps: [SPECIFIC ACTIONS AND TIMELINE]

Tone: Professional but warm
Length: 2-3 paragraphs
Include: Clear call-to-action for [NEXT ACTION]
```

### After Proposal
```
Draft a follow-up email to [CLIENT NAME] one week after submitting our [PROJECT TYPE] proposal.

Express continued interest in working together.
Offer to answer any questions about:
- [ASPECT 1 - e.g., timeline]
- [ASPECT 2 - e.g., pricing]
- [ASPECT 3 - e.g., team structure]

Suggest a brief call to discuss.

Tone: Enthusiastic but not pushy
Length: 2 paragraphs maximum
```

### After No Response
```
Draft a polite follow-up email to [RECIPIENT] who hasn't responded to my [PREVIOUS EMAIL TYPE] sent [TIME PERIOD] ago.

Reference the original email about [TOPIC].
Acknowledge they may be busy.
Briefly restate the [KEY REQUEST OR QUESTION].
Offer alternative: [ALTERNATIVE ACTION].

Tone: Understanding and professional, not demanding
Length: Short - 1-2 paragraphs
```

## Customer Communication

### Welcome Email
```
Draft a welcome email for new customers who just [SIGNED UP/PURCHASED/JOINED].

Welcome them to [COMPANY/PRODUCT/SERVICE].
Highlight these immediate benefits:
- [BENEFIT 1]
- [BENEFIT 2]
- [BENEFIT 3]

Provide these next steps:
1. [STEP 1]
2. [STEP 2]
3. [STEP 3]

Include: Link to [RESOURCE] and contact information for support

Tone: Friendly and helpful
Length: 3-4 paragraphs
```

### Customer Support Response
```
Draft a customer service response to this inquiry/complaint:

[PASTE CUSTOMER EMAIL]

Acknowledge: [THEIR CONCERN]
Apologize for: [SPECIFIC ISSUE]
Explain: [BRIEF EXPLANATION - keep it simple]
Offer solution: [SPECIFIC RESOLUTION]
Provide: [COMPENSATION IF APPLICABLE]

Tone: Empathetic and solution-focused
Length: 3 paragraphs
Include: Direct contact information and next steps
```

### Thank You Email
```
Draft a thank-you email to [CUSTOMER NAME] for [SPECIFIC ACTION - purchase, referral, feedback, etc.].

Express genuine appreciation for [WHAT THEY DID].
Mention specific impact: [HOW IT HELPED].
[OPTIONAL: Offer incentive/discount for future]

Tone: Warm and sincere, not overly formal
Length: 2 paragraphs
```

## Internal Communication

### Status Update
```
Draft a [WEEKLY/MONTHLY] status update email for [PROJECT NAME].

Audience: [MANAGER/TEAM/STAKEHOLDERS]

Include:
- Progress this [PERIOD]: [KEY ACCOMPLISHMENTS]
- Current status: [ON TRACK/AT RISK/BEHIND]
- Blockers: [ANY ISSUES]
- Next [PERIOD] plans: [UPCOMING WORK]
- Help needed: [IF ANY]

Format: Bullet points for easy scanning
Tone: Professional and factual
Length: Maximum 200 words
```

### Team Announcement
```
Draft an announcement email to [TEAM/DEPARTMENT/COMPANY] about [TOPIC].

Announce: [WHAT'S CHANGING/HAPPENING]
Explain why: [REASON/BENEFIT]
Detail what this means: [IMPACT ON TEAM]
Provide timeline: [WHEN THIS TAKES EFFECT]
Next steps: [WHAT PEOPLE SHOULD DO]

Tone: [POSITIVE/INFORMATIVE/REASSURING]
Length: 3-4 paragraphs
Include: FAQ section if needed
```

### Meeting Request
```
Draft a meeting request email to [RECIPIENT(S)] to discuss [TOPIC].

Purpose: [SPECIFIC MEETING OBJECTIVE]
Proposed duration: [TIME]
Suggested times: [OPTIONS]
Agenda items:
- [ITEM 1]
- [ITEM 2]
- [ITEM 3]

Preparation needed: [WHAT TO REVIEW BEFOREHAND]

Tone: Professional and respectful of their time
Length: 2 paragraphs + agenda
```

## Sales and Marketing

### Cold Outreach
```
Draft a cold outreach email to [PROSPECT NAME/TITLE] at [COMPANY].

Introduce: [YOUR COMPANY] and [YOUR ROLE]
Mention: [RELEVANT CONNECTION/RESEARCH POINT]
Explain how we help: [SPECIFIC VALUE PROPOSITION FOR THEIR INDUSTRY]
Reference: [SIMILAR CLIENT SUCCESS IF APPLICABLE]

Call-to-action: [15-MINUTE CALL/DEMO/MEETING]

Tone: Professional, helpful (not salesy)
Length: 3 short paragraphs
Subject line: [COMPELLING AND RELEVANT]
```

### Product Introduction
```
Draft a product introduction email for [PRODUCT NAME] to [TARGET AUDIENCE].

Highlight problem it solves: [PAIN POINT]
Key features:
- [FEATURE 1 with benefit]
- [FEATURE 2 with benefit]
- [FEATURE 3 with benefit]

Special offer: [IF APPLICABLE]
Call-to-action: [FREE TRIAL/DEMO/LEARN MORE]

Tone: Enthusiastic but professional
Length: 3-4 paragraphs
Include: Clear next step button/link
```

### Event Invitation
```
Draft an invitation email for [EVENT NAME] on [DATE].

Event details:
- What: [EVENT TYPE AND TOPIC]
- When: [DATE AND TIME]
- Where: [LOCATION/VIRTUAL]
- Who should attend: [TARGET AUDIENCE]

Why attend:
- [BENEFIT 1]
- [BENEFIT 2]
- [BENEFIT 3]

RSVP: [DEADLINE AND METHOD]

Tone: Inviting and exciting
Length: 2-3 paragraphs
Include: Calendar invite attachment
```

## Tips for Using These Templates

1. **Customize placeholders** - Replace all [BRACKETED TEXT] with your specific information
2. **Adjust tone** - Modify tone specification based on your relationship and context
3. **Adapt length** - Change word/paragraph counts based on your needs
4. **Add context** - Include relevant background information for better results
5. **Iterate** - If first output isn't perfect, refine the prompt and try again
6. **Save successful prompts** - Build your personal library of what works

## Validation Prompt for Emails

Use this to check any email generated by AI:

```
Please review this email I generated using AI:

**Purpose**: [What this email is meant to accomplish]

**Email Content**:
[Paste the generated email]

**Please assess**:
1. Is the tone appropriate for the recipient and situation?
2. Is the message clear and easy to understand?
3. Does it include all necessary information?
4. Is the call-to-action clear?
5. Is the length appropriate?
6. Are there any potential issues or improvements?

Provide specific feedback and suggestions.
```
