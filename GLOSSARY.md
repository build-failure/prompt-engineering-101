# Glossary of Prompt Engineering Terms

A comprehensive reference of key terms and concepts used throughout this course.

---

## A

**AI (Artificial Intelligence)**
Computer systems that can perform tasks typically requiring human intelligence, such as understanding language, recognizing patterns, and making decisions.

**Ambiguity**
Unclear or vague language in a prompt that can be interpreted in multiple ways, often leading to unexpected AI outputs.

---

## B

**Baseline Prompt**
A simple, initial version of a prompt before optimization or refinement.

**Business Context**
Background information about your organization, industry, or specific situation that helps AI generate more relevant outputs.

---

## C

**Chain of Thought**
A technique where you ask AI to show its reasoning process step-by-step, improving accuracy for complex tasks.

**Constraint**
A limitation or requirement specified in a prompt (e.g., word count, tone, format, what to avoid).

**Context**
Background information provided in a prompt to help AI understand the situation and generate appropriate responses.

**Context Window**
The amount of text (input + output) that an AI model can process in a single interaction. Gemini has a large context window.

---

## D

**Delimiter**
A character or symbol used to separate different parts of a prompt (e.g., triple quotes """, dashes ---, or XML-style tags).

**Direct Instruction**
Clear, explicit commands telling AI exactly what to do (e.g., "Summarize this in 3 bullet points").

---

## E

**Example-Based Learning**
See "Few-Shot Learning"

**Expected Output**
A description or sample of what you want the AI to produce, often included in lab exercises for comparison.

---

## F

**Few-Shot Learning**
Providing AI with 2-5 examples of desired input-output pairs to teach it a pattern or style.

**Format**
The structure or layout you want for AI output (e.g., bullet list, table, paragraph, email format).

---

## G

**Gemini**
Google's large language model and AI assistant, the primary tool used in this course.

**Generative AI**
AI systems that can create new content (text, images, code) based on prompts and training data.

---

## H

**Hallucination**
When AI generates false, fabricated, or nonsensical information presented as fact. A key challenge in prompt engineering.

---

## I

**Instruction**
The part of a prompt that tells AI what action to perform (e.g., "summarize," "analyze," "draft").

**Instruction-Data Separation**
A technique of clearly dividing the instructions from the data being processed, improving prompt clarity.

**Iteration**
The process of refining and improving a prompt through multiple attempts based on results.

---

## L

**Large Language Model (LLM)**
An AI system trained on vast amounts of text data to understand and generate human-like language. Gemini is an LLM.

---

## M

**Multi-Step Workflow**
Breaking a complex task into a sequence of simpler prompts, where each step builds on the previous one.

---

## O

**One-Shot Learning**
Providing AI with a single example to demonstrate the desired pattern or format.

**Output**
The response or content generated by AI based on your prompt.

---

## P

**Persona**
A role or character assigned to AI (e.g., "act as a marketing expert") to influence its perspective and output style.

**Prompt**
The input text you provide to AI, containing instructions, context, and any necessary data.

**Prompt Chaining**
Linking multiple prompts together in sequence, using the output of one as input for the next.

**Prompt Engineering**
The practice of designing effective prompts to get desired outputs from AI systems.

**Prompt Template**
A reusable prompt structure with placeholders that can be customized for different situations.

---

## R

**Refinement**
The process of improving a prompt based on initial results, making it more specific or better structured.

**Role Assignment**
Specifying what role or expertise AI should adopt (e.g., "You are a data analyst").

---

## S

**Specificity**
The level of detail and precision in a prompt. More specific prompts generally produce better results.

**Step-by-Step Reasoning**
See "Chain of Thought"

**Structure**
The organization and format of a prompt, typically including task, context, format, and constraints (TCFC framework).

**System Prompt**
Initial instructions that set AI's behavior for an entire conversation (less relevant for single-interaction use).

---

## T

**Task**
The specific action you want AI to perform (e.g., summarize, analyze, draft, compare).

**TCFC Framework**
A prompt structure model: Task, Context, Format, Constraints. Covered in Chapter 2.

**Temperature**
A setting that controls randomness in AI outputs (higher = more creative, lower = more focused). Not directly controllable in Gemini's standard interface.

**Token**
A unit of text (roughly a word or word part) that AI processes. Context windows are measured in tokens.

**Tone**
The style or attitude of AI's output (e.g., professional, casual, empathetic, authoritative).

---

## V

**Validation Prompt**
A special prompt used to assess whether your work meets the exercise objectives, used for self-assessment in labs.

**Vague Language**
Imprecise words or phrases that can be interpreted in multiple ways (e.g., "some," "good," "soon").

---

## Z

**Zero-Shot Learning**
Asking AI to perform a task without providing any examples, relying on its training alone.

---

## Common Acronyms

**AI** - Artificial Intelligence
**LLM** - Large Language Model
**NLP** - Natural Language Processing
**TCFC** - Task, Context, Format, Constraints

## Related Terms Not Covered in This Course

These terms are relevant to AI but beyond the scope of this beginner-to-intermediate course:

- **API (Application Programming Interface)** - Technical integration method
- **Embeddings** - Numerical representations of text
- **Fine-tuning** - Customizing AI models with additional training
- **Model Parameters** - Internal AI model settings
- **RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)** - Advanced technique combining search and generation
- **Tokenization** - How text is split into tokens
